## Phase 1: Initial Structure (Early Rounds)

### Core Philosophy and 3-Variable System Choice
We chose to evolve a **3-variable system** in Newtonian gauge:
- `δφ` — PRTOE scalar field perturbation
- `Φ` — Gravitational potential (Bardeen variable)
- `η = Ψ − Φ` — Slip parameter (directly sourced by anisotropic stress from the non-minimal coupling)

This choice was made for the following reasons:
- Newtonian gauge is the standard and most convenient gauge for implementation in CLASS.
- The slip `η` is physically generated by the `F(φ)R` term at linear order, so evolving it directly makes the anisotropic stress effect transparent.
- It avoids unnecessary gauge transformations while still capturing all physical degrees of freedom at linear order.
- The system is closed: we evolve `δφ` via its perturbed Klein-Gordon equation, `Φ` via the second-order Einstein equation, and `η` via the slip evolution equation (sourced by `π_PRTOE`).

### Earliest Proposed Form of the Perturbed Klein-Gordon Equation (Round 1)
In the very first derivations, the KG equation was written with many terms still grouped:

```math
\delta\phi'' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + [k^2 + a^2 m_{\rm eff}^2] \delta\phi = - \frac{F_\phi}{F} \times (\text{metric sources})
```

Where the effective mass at that stage was:

```math
m_{\rm eff}^2 = V_{\phi\phi} + \frac{F_{\phi\phi}}{F} \phi'^2 - 3 \frac{F_\phi}{F} (\mathcal{H}' + 2\mathcal{H}^2) a^2
```

**Explanation**: At this early stage, the right-hand side still contained schematic "metric sources". The contributions from `δF · R`, `∇_μ ∇_ν δF`, and the mixing between `F_φ` and the metric perturbations (Φ, η) were not yet expanded term by term. The third-derivative terms (`F_{\phi\phi\phi}`) were not yet visible.

### First Version of the Slip Evolution Equation (Early Rounds)
An early form of the slip equation was:

```math
\eta'' + 3\mathcal{H} \eta' + k^2 \eta = \frac{F_\phi}{F} (\delta\phi'' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + k^2 \delta\phi) + \text{higher mixing terms}
```

**Explanation**: This version already identified that the non-minimal coupling sources slip, but the anisotropic stress `π_PRTOE` was not yet written in its clean projected form. The term `-k²/3 δφ` (the traceless projection) was missing at this stage.

## Phase 2: Evolution of the Slip Equation and Anisotropic Stress

### Key Challenge
The non-minimal coupling `F(φ)R` generates anisotropic stress at linear order. This is one of the most important physical effects of the model because it directly sources the gravitational slip `η = Ψ − Φ`. Getting the form of `π_PRTOE` correct was therefore critical.

### Version 1 (Early Rounds)
An early form of the slip evolution equation was:

```math
\eta'' + 3\mathcal{H} \eta' + k^2 \eta = \frac{F_\phi}{F} (\delta\phi'' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + k^2 \delta\phi) + \text{higher mixing terms}
```

**Explanation**: This version correctly identified that the PRTOE sector sources slip, but the anisotropic stress was not yet written in its clean, projected form. The important traceless projection term `-k²/3 δφ` was missing.

### Version 2 (Mid Rounds — After Adding Anisotropic Stress)
After realizing the importance of the traceless part, an improved version appeared:

```math
\eta'' + 3\mathcal{H} \eta' + k^2 \eta = \frac{F_\phi}{F} \left( \delta\phi'' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi' - \frac{k^2}{3} \delta\phi \right) 
+ \frac{F_{\phi\phi} \phi'}{F} \left( \delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi \right) + \text{other mixing}
```

**Explanation**: The `-k²/3 δφ` term (the traceless projection of the scalar perturbation) was added. This made the equation more consistent with the (0,i) momentum constraint.

### Version 3 (Final Form)
The final, clean form of the slip evolution equation (used in the reference) is:

```math
\begin{aligned}
\eta'' + 3\mathcal{H} \eta' + k^2 \eta 
&= \frac{2 F_\phi}{F} \Big( \delta\phi'' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + k^2 \delta\phi \Big) \\
&\quad + \frac{F_{\phi\phi}}{F} \Big( \delta\phi'' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi' \Big) \\
&\quad + \frac{F_\phi}{F} \left( \delta\phi'' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi' - \frac{k^2}{3} \delta\phi \right) \\
&\quad + \frac{F_{\phi\phi} \phi'}{F} \left( \delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi \right) \\
&\quad + \frac{3 a^2}{F} (\rho_m + p_m) \frac{\theta_m}{k^2}
\end{aligned}
```

**Anisotropic Stress (Final Explicit Form)**:
```math
\pi_{\rm PRTOE} = \frac{F_\phi}{F} \left( \delta\phi'' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi' - \frac{k^2}{3} \delta\phi \right) 
+ \frac{F_{\phi\phi} \phi'}{F} \left( \delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi \right)
```

**Explanation of final improvements**:
- Multiple `F_φ` and `F_φφ` contributions were collected and organized.
- The term proportional to `θ_m` (matter velocity divergence) was added for consistency with the full Einstein equations.
- This final form is consistent with both the field picture and the effective fluid picture.

## Phase 3: Refinement of the Second-Order Φ Equation

### Main Difficulty
The source term on the right-hand side of the Φ equation receives contributions from both matter and the PRTOE sector. Several terms arise from the variation of `δF · R`.

### Intermediate Source Term
Earlier versions had more schematic source terms such as:

```math
S_\Phi = \frac{a^2}{2F} (\delta\rho_m' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\rho_m + \text{PRTOE contributions})
```

**Explanation of improvement**:
We expanded the PRTOE contributions explicitly, leading to the final form:

```math
S_\Phi = \frac{a^2}{2F} \Bigg(
    \delta\rho_m' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\rho_m 
    + \frac{F_\phi}{F} (\delta\phi'' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + k^2 \delta\phi)
    + \frac{R F_\phi}{2F} (\delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi)
    + \frac{F_{\phi\phi} \phi'}{F} (\delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi)
\Bigg)
```

The term `R F_φ / 2F` was carefully verified as the correct coefficient coming from the `δF · R` variation in Newtonian gauge.

## Phase 4: Evolution of the Momentum Constraint (0,i)

### Why the Momentum Constraint Was Critical
Many source terms that appear in the second-order equation for Φ are obtained by taking the time derivative of the (0,0) Einstein equation and then using the (0,i) momentum constraint to eliminate certain terms. Therefore, the momentum constraint had to be made explicit and consistent with the field equations.

### Version 1 (Early — Highly Schematic)
In the earliest derivations, the momentum constraint was written with several terms left schematic:

```math
k^2 (\Phi' + \mathcal{H} \Psi) = \frac{a^2}{2F} \Big[ 
    (\rho_m + p_m) \theta_m 
    + \frac{F_\phi}{F} (\delta\phi'' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + k^2 \delta\phi) 
    + \text{PRTOE mixing terms} 
    + \text{small velocity mixing terms}
\Big]
```

**Explanation**: At this stage, we knew the structure (matter velocity + PRTOE contributions from `F_φ` and metric mixing), but many coefficients and higher-derivative mixing terms were not yet expanded.

### Version 2 (Mid Rounds — More Explicit)
After further derivation work, the momentum constraint was improved to:

```math
k^2 (\Phi' + \mathcal{H} \Psi) = \frac{a^2}{2F} \Bigg[
    (\rho_m + p_m) \theta_m 
    + \frac{F_\phi}{F} (\delta\phi'' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + k^2 \delta\phi)
    + \frac{F_{\phi\phi} \phi'}{F} (\delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi)
    + \frac{R F_\phi}{2F} (\delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi)
    + \frac{F_{\phi\phi\phi} \phi' \phi''}{F} \delta\phi 
    + \text{velocity gradient mixing from } \delta p_{\rm PRTOE}
\Bigg]
```

**Explanation**: The `F_{\phi\phi}` and `R F_φ` terms were made explicit. The remaining schematic piece was the velocity gradient mixing coming from the effective pressure perturbation `δp_PRTOE`.

### Version 3 (Final — Fully Explicit)
The final version removed the last schematic piece:

```math
k^2 (\Phi' + \mathcal{H} \Psi) = \frac{a^2}{2F} \Bigg[
    (\rho_m + p_m) \theta_m 
    + \frac{F_\phi}{F} (\delta\phi'' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi' + k^2 \delta\phi)
    + \frac{F_{\phi\phi} \phi'}{F} (\delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi)
    + \frac{R F_\phi}{2F} (\delta\phi' + \mathcal{H} \delta\phi)
    + \frac{F_{\phi\phi\phi} \phi' \phi''}{F} \delta\phi 
    + \frac{F_\phi}{F} \Big( \delta p'_{\rm PRTOE} + \mathcal{H} \delta p_{\rm PRTOE} \Big) \text{ gradient projection}
\Bigg]
```

**Explanation**: The velocity gradient mixing term was identified as the gradient projection of the time derivative of the effective pressure perturbation. This made the momentum constraint fully explicit and consistent with the other equations.

## Phase 4b: Effective Fluid Equations (Derived in Round 3)

During the refinement process, we also derived the effective imperfect fluid description of the PRTOE sector. This was done both for physical insight and for cross-validation.

### Effective Density Perturbation
```math
\delta\rho_{\rm PRTOE} = \dot{\phi} \delta\dot{\phi} 
+ V_\phi \delta\phi 
+ \frac{R}{2} F_\phi \delta\phi 
+ 3H F_\phi \delta\phi 
+ \text{kinetic mixing terms from } F_\phi \text{ variation}
```

### Continuity Equation for the Effective Fluid (Intermediate Form)
```math
\begin{aligned}
\delta\rho_{\rm PRTOE}' + 3\mathcal{H} (\delta\rho_{\rm PRTOE} + \delta p_{\rm PRTOE}) 
+ (\rho_{\rm PRTOE} + p_{\rm PRTOE}) \big( \theta_{\rm PRTOE} + 3\Phi' \big) \\
= \frac{F_\phi}{F} \Big( \text{terms involving } \delta\phi'', \delta\phi', \delta\phi \Big)
\end{aligned}
```

**Explanation**: The right-hand side contains source terms coming from the non-minimal coupling. The effective fluid is **not** independently conserved — it exchanges energy-momentum with the gravitational sector.

### Euler Equation for the Effective Fluid (Intermediate Form)
```math
\begin{aligned}
\theta_{\rm PRTOE}' + \mathcal{H} \theta_{\rm PRTOE} 
+ \frac{k^2}{a^2} \frac{\delta p_{\rm PRTOE}}{\rho_{\rm PRTOE} + p_{\rm PRTOE}} 
+ k^2 \Psi 
+ \frac{k^2}{a^2} \frac{\pi_{\rm PRTOE}}{\rho_{\rm PRTOE} + p_{\rm PRTOE}} \\
= \text{additional mixing terms from } F_\phi \text{ and } F_{\phi\phi}
\end{aligned}
```

**Explanation**: The term involving `π_PRTOE` (anisotropic stress) is one of the main ways the slip `η` is sourced in the fluid picture. These effective fluid equations were derived for completeness and cross-checks, but the primary implementation path remains evolving `δφ` directly.

## Phase 5: Effective Fluid Description

During the process, we also derived an effective imperfect fluid picture for the PRTOE sector.

### Effective Density Perturbation (Intermediate → Final)
Early form was schematic. The more complete form is:

```math
\delta\rho_{\rm PRTOE} = \dot{\phi} \delta\dot{\phi} 
+ V_\phi \delta\phi 
+ \frac{R}{2} F_\phi \delta\phi 
+ 3H F_\phi \delta\phi 
+ F_\phi \left( \delta\phi'' + 3\mathcal{H} \delta\phi' \right) / a^2
```

### Effective Pressure and Anisotropic Stress
These were derived to ensure consistency between the field picture and the fluid picture. The anisotropic stress `π_PRTOE` is especially important because it directly sources the slip.

**Note**: The effective fluid continuity and Euler equations were derived but left partially schematic in some source terms. These are two of the remaining gaps for symbolic verification.

## Phase 6: Third-Derivative Terms (`F_{\phi\phi\phi}`)

In later rounds, we encountered terms involving the third derivative of the coupling function.

**Explanation**:
These terms appear in the perturbed Klein-Gordon equation and (weakly) in the Φ source. They are numerically suppressed when:
- The background field evolves slowly (`φ''` and higher derivatives small), and
- The coupling derivatives `F_φ`, `F_φφ`, `F_φφφ` are not extremely large.

Because of this suppression, they were kept in grouped form in the final reference, with a note that full expansion is recommended for publication-level rigor.
